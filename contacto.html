About Me

Hello, I’m Alexis, a backend Java developer in training. I created The Virtual Garage as a personal project to combine two passions: web development and the automotive world. The idea was to build a space that showcases iconic car brands, paying attention to both visual presentation and technical structure.

About This Project

This website is a personal project aimed at improving my skills in web development. It draws heavy inspiration from early 2000s automotive websites such as MotorZone, Drive & Track, and Top Gear. I wanted to capture that nostalgic, bold, and slightly raw aesthetic while applying modern coding practices for structure, maintainability, and responsiveness.

Development Time & Learning Process

Building The Virtual Garage took me approximately three weeks from concept to completion. During this time, I deepened my understanding of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, learned to structure projects for scalability, and refined my approach to reusable templates and modular CSS. I also practiced integrating interactive elements like image rotators, custom marquees, and brand-specific styling while keeping the codebase organized and easy to maintain.
